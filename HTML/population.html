Population: of Australia





The graph below shows the population growth of Australians over time. At the turn of the 20th century, the country started with approximately 4 million people. This number was slow to grow for the first 50 years. One could infer that this is consistent with global trends at that time. The first half of the 20th century witnessed 2 major world wars and a global pandemic called the Spanish Flu.



After the first 50 years, there's a sudden spur of growth. This is an interesting observation which is consistent with Australian's change in foreign policy. Australia opened its doors wide open to attract the best and brightest to its shores. This new policy saw a steady increase of population. It increased the diversity and make-up of Australians.
